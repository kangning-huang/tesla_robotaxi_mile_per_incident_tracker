Understanding the Numbers

What counts as an "incident"?

Under NHTSA Standing General Order 2021-01, Tesla must report ANY crash where the autonomous driving system was engaged within 30 seconds — including minor fender-benders that human drivers would never report.

Why two human driver benchmarks?

  • 500K (Police Reports): Official NHTSA data, but undercounts ~60% of crashes
  • 300K (Insurance Claims): More complete picture from Swiss Re/Waymo study

The apples-to-apples problem

Tesla reports everything. Humans report almost nothing minor. The 300K insurance benchmark is a fairer comparison.

Crash Rate FAQ

Common questions about Tesla Robotaxi crash rates and safety data

Tesla Robotaxi's latest crash rate is 1 incident per 92,500 miles driven. This has improved from 1 per 14,100 miles at launch in June 2025. The crash rate is calculated from NHTSA Standing General Order reports, which require Tesla to report any crash within 30 seconds of autonomous system engagement.

Tesla has reported 10 incidents to NHTSA since the Austin robotaxi launch in June 2025. All incidents occurred in Austin, TX, which is the only location with unsupervised Level 4 autonomous driving. The interval between incidents has been steadily increasing.

Tesla Robotaxi currently achieves 92,500 miles between incidents, compared to human drivers who average 500,000 miles between police-reported crashes or 300,000 miles between insurance claims. Tesla is currently 5.4x worse than police-reported human rates, but improving rapidly with safety doubling every 69 days.
