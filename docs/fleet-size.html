Fleet Size FAQ

Common questions about Tesla's robotaxi fleet size and growth

As of January 2026, Tesla operates over 500 robotaxi vehicles across Austin, TX and the San Francisco Bay Area. The Austin unsupervised fleet (Level 4 autonomy, no safety driver) has grown from 10 to 72 vehicles since June 2025. The Bay Area fleet of approximately 164 vehicles operates with safety drivers under California regulations.

According to Elon Musk at the Q4 2025 earnings call, Tesla's robotaxi fleet is expected to double every month. The Austin unsupervised fleet grew from 10 vehicles in June 2025 to 72 vehicles by January 2026 — a 7x increase in 7 months.

Austin, TX has approximately 72 unsupervised Tesla Cybercab vehicles operating without safety drivers as of January 2026. This is the only location where Tesla operates true Level 4 autonomous vehicles that must report incidents to NHTSA.

The San Francisco Bay Area has approximately 164 Tesla vehicles operating with safety drivers, as required by California regulations. These operate under Level 2 ADAS rules, not the Level 4 ADS requirements that apply to Austin's unsupervised fleet.

Tesla has announced plans to expand robotaxi operations to Dallas, Houston, Phoenix, Miami, Orlando, Tampa, and Las Vegas in the first half of 2026.
